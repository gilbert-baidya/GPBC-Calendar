Our History

Grace and Praise Bangladeshi Church was founded with a vision to create a spiritual home for the Bangladeshi community in California. Our journey began with a small group of believers who gathered to worship God and celebrate their cultural heritage.

Over the years, we have grown into a vibrant community that embraces both our Bangladeshi roots and our identity as followers of Christ. We continue to welcome families from all backgrounds who seek to grow in their faith and serve God together.

Our church has become a beacon of hope, love, and spiritual growth in our community, hosting regular services, cultural celebrations, and outreach programs that touch lives and spread God's love.

Our Mission

"Passion for God, Compassion for People"

Our mission is to glorify God by making disciples who love Jesus, love each other, and love our community. We are committed to:

  • Worshipping God with all our hearts in both English and Bangla
  • Building strong, Christ-centered families
  • Equipping believers for ministry and service
  • Reaching our community with the Gospel of Jesus Christ
  • Celebrating our Bangladeshi heritage while embracing all cultures
  • Serving the needs of our local and global community

Our Beliefs

We believe in the fundamental truths of the Christian faith:

  • The Bible: We believe the Bible is the inspired, infallible Word of God and our ultimate authority for faith and life.
  • The Trinity: We believe in one God eternally existing in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.
  • Jesus Christ: We believe Jesus Christ is the Son of God, born of a virgin, lived a sinless life, died for our sins, rose from the dead, and will return again.
  • Salvation: We believe salvation is by grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone.
  • The Church: We believe in the universal Church, the body of Christ, made up of all true believers.
  • Baptism & Communion: We practice believer's baptism and regular communion as ordinances established by Christ.
  • Eternal Life: We believe in the resurrection of both the saved and the lost‚Äîthe saved to eternal life and the lost to eternal separation from God.
